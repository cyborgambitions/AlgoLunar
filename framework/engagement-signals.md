# Engagement Signals from the X Algorithm (Phoenix)
This document summarizes the key signals the X recommendation algorithm (Phoenix) rewards, based on the open-sourced code. Use this as a reference when creating content for the Lunar Economist brand.
## Core Principle
The **Phoenix model** (X’s current recommendation algorithm) works by predicting the probability that a user will take specific actions on a post.
Instead of relying on simple like counts, it evaluates how likely someone is to:
- Reply
- Repost
- Spend time reading
- Visit the author’s profile
- Follow the author
**Content that increases the probability of high-value positive actions** — especially **replies** and **profile visits/follows** — receives significantly better distribution in the For You feed.
This is why generic or low-effort posts underperform, while original, insightful, and conversation-sparking content tends to gain more reach.
## High-Value Signals to Optimize For
### 1. Replies (Very High Impact)
- The algorithm gives strong weight to replies.
- **Tactic**: End threads with a clear, thoughtful question. Ask for opinions, predictions, or experiences.
- **Example**: "Will helium-3 become the backbone of the lunar economy? What do you think?"
### 2. Profile Visits & Follows (Extremely High Impact)
- Making someone visit your profile and follow you is one of the strongest positive signals.- - **Tactic**: Deliver high-value, insightful content that positions you as an authority in the lunar economy space. Use strong hooks and consistent branding.
### 3. Media (Images, Charts, Visuals)
- Posts with images and charts increase dwell time and engagement probability.
- **Tactic**: Include relevant charts, data visualizations, or simple graphics in threads about lunar economics and infrastructure.- **Tactic**: Include relevant charts, data visualizations, or simple graphics in threads about lunar economics and infrastructure.
### 4. Semantic Relevance & Originality
- The transformer model understands the *meaning* of your content.
- **Tactic**: Write original, specific, and substantive content. Avoid generic or AI-sounding posts. Focus on clear lunar economy angles.
### 5. Early Engagement Velocity
- The first 30–60 minutes are critical. Strong early engagement helps the post get amplified.
The Phoenix model predicts the probability of multiple user actions. Content that increases the likelihood of **positive actions** (especially replies and follows) gets higher reach.
- **Tactic**: Post when your target audience is most active. Reply quickly to early comments to boost momentum.
### 6. Dwell Time
- Time users spend reading your content matters.
- **Tactic**: Write valuable, well-structured threads that encourage people to read the full thread.
## Signals That Usually Hurt Reach
- Posting too frequently from the same account (author diversity penalty)
- Low-quality, generic, or repetitive content
- Negative user actions (blocks, mutes, reports)
- Putting external links in the main post
